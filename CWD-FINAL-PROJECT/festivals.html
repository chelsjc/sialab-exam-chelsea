Moriones Festival

The Moriones Festival is a vibrant and deeply-rooted cultural celebration that takes place in the Philippines during Holy Week. This remarkable festival is specifically held on the island of Marinduque, known for its rich history and captivating traditions. The Moriones Festival is a unique and colorful event that combines religious devotion with theatrical performances, making it a must-see cultural experience for both locals and tourists.

Panagbenga Festival

The Panagbenga Festival, often referred to as the "Flower Festival," is a month-long annual event held in Baguio City, Philippines. This festival is one of the most celebrated and well-attended flower festivals in the country and showcases the rich culture and vibrant traditions of the region. The term "Panagbenga" means "season of blooming" in the local Kankanaey language.

Higantes Festival

The Higantes Festival is a unique and colorful celebration held in Angono, Rizal, Philippines. This annual event is known for its giant papier-mâché effigies, locally called "higantes," which are paraded through the streets of Angono. The festival is a joyful and lively representation of the town's culture, history, and creativity.

Close

Pahiyas Festival

The Pahiyas Festival is an annual cultural event held in Lucban, Quezon, Philippines. This colorful and vibrant festival is celebrated to honor and give thanks for a bountiful harvest. The Pahiyas Festival is known for its elaborate and creative decorations that adorn houses, turning the town into a visual feast of colors and artistry.

Sinulog Festival

The Sinulog Festival is a grand and widely celebrated cultural and religious event in the Philippines, specifically in Cebu City. This annual festival honors the Santo Niño, or the Child Jesus, and is known for its lively street dances, vibrant costumes, and rich cultural and religious traditions.

Close

Dinagyang Festival

The Dinagyang Festival is an electrifying and colorful cultural event celebrated in Iloilo City, Philippines. This annual festival pays homage to the Santo Niño, or the Child Jesus, and is renowned for its dynamic street performances, vibrant costumes, and fervent religious devotion.

Close

Masskara Festival

The Masskara Festival is a lively and exuberant annual event celebrated in Bacolod City, Philippines. Known for its vibrant masks and colorful costumes, this festival is a symbol of the resilience and optimism of the people of Bacolod, even in the face of challenges and adversity. The festival is a fusion of art, culture, and celebration.

Close

Pintados Festival

The Pintados Festival is a cultural celebration held in Tacloban City, Leyte, Philippines. This festival is characterized by body painting, tattooing, and elaborate traditional costumes, paying tribute to the region's ancient cultural practices and the bravery of the Pintados warriors.

Lanzones Festival

The Lanzones Festival is an annual cultural celebration held in Camiguin, an island province in the Philippines. The festival revolves around the lanzones fruit, a tropical fruit that is abundant in the region. It is a lively and colorful event that combines religious traditions with local culture and the bountiful harvest of lanzones.

Close

Tuna Festival

The Tuna Festival is an annual cultural and trade celebration held in General Santos City, located in the southern part of the Philippines. The festival pays tribute to the city's tuna industry, which is a significant source of livelihood and an important sector of the local economy.

Close

Balangay Festival

The Balangay Festival is an annual cultural event celebrated in Butuan City, in the Caraga region of the Philippines. The festival is named after the "balangay," which is an ancient boat traditionally used by the indigenous people in the region. The festival honors the city's rich cultural heritage and its historical significance as the site where the oldest pre-colonial boat in Southeast Asia was discovered.

Close

Sibugay Festival

The Sibugay Festival is an annual cultural event celebrated in the municipality of Zamboanguita in Negros Oriental, Philippines. The festival primarily pays homage to the town's patron saint, Saint James the Greater, and showcases the rich marine resources of the area, as well as the local culture and traditions.
